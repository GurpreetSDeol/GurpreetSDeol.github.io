  • MSc Final Project: Football Analysis using Principal Component Analysis

    Download MSc Thesis

    Analysed 13 years of football data from Europe's top five leagues (Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, Bundesliga, Ligue 1) to uncover tactical trends and shifts in playing styles. Applied machine learning techniques including Principal Component Analysis and K-Means clustering to examine team performance in areas such as defense, passing, and finishing. Identified a league-wide evolution toward possession-based play, while also comparing competitiveness and stylistic variance across leagues. Key findings highlighted La Liga’s duopoly, Serie A’s competitiveness, and emerging title contenders in the Premier League.

  • BSc Physics Final Year Project: Simulations of Electrical Resistance in 2 Dimensions

    Download BSc Thesis

    Simulated the resistance of 2D conductors with arbitrary geometries using Python and the finite difference method. Applied a 100V potential across two ohmic contacts to numerically solve for the electric potential and electric field distribution. The project explored how shape and boundary conditions influence current flow and overall resistance in conductive materials.
